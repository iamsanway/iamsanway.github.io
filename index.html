Research

Quantum sensing with rubidium atoms in solid neon

My PhD research focuses on developing quantum sensors based on rubidium atoms embedded in solid neon. The long-term goal is to use individual atoms as nanoscale magnetic sensors capable of detecting nearby nuclear spins and eventually imaging molecules at the nanoscale.

In the experiment, rubidium atoms are optically initialized and read out using laser-induced fluorescence. RF control is used to drive transitions between spin states, and dynamical decoupling pulse sequences are applied to make the sensor sensitive to weak oscillating magnetic fields from nearby nuclei.

My current work involves coherent control, optical pumping and readout, photon-counting measurements, pulse-sequence optimization, NMR detection, and improving the experimental platform toward single-atom sensing.
